The Milanese
Invasion

The Historical Festival of Como

⚔

In the year 1127, Milan's army came to Como and left only ruins. Nine hundred years later, the Milanese come back — but this time they bring food, music, and fire. Three days of living history inside and around the old city walls, where the past is not read but lived, tasted, and celebrated together.

The Three Acts of the Festival

I Day One · Friday
The Drama

The invasion begins. Every visitor gets a medieval passport. The city walls come alive with AR history, fire shows, and the curfew of 1127 re-acted after dark.

II Day Two · Saturday
The Contest

Milan is inside the walls — but the war becomes competition. Cooking duels, archery contests, craft markets, sports challenges, and history workshops fill every street.

III Day Three · Sunday
The Fusion

The walls become one long dinner table. A big shared meal runs along the old city centre. Video mapping turns the ancient stones into a screen of light. The night ends in fireworks.

What makes this festival special
Key Features
🎫
Medieval Passport System

Every visitor gets a paper passport at the entrance. If you enter from Porta Torre you are stamped as an Invader from Milan. If you are already inside you become a Defender of Como. No tasks — just atmosphere and identity.

📱
Augmented Reality Walls

QR codes are placed along the old stone walls. Scan them with your phone to see a short AR video of the 1127 siege. Walls fall, soldiers charge — right where you are standing. History becomes real.

🎧
Silent Disco Audio Guides

Instead of boring information boards, you wear wireless headphones. As you walk the walls you hear the sounds of the siege: shouting, battering rams, war drums. The audio changes as you move.

🪙
The Sesterzio — Festival Coin

At the entrance you exchange real euros for Sesterzi Comaschi, a special festival coin. You use it at all food stalls and workshops. Any coins you do not spend can be refunded at the exit or kept as a souvenir.

🏹
Como vs. Milan Challenges

Real competitions across three days — archery, cooking duels, football, and craft skill tests. Local teams from Como compete against visiting Milanese challengers. The rivalry grows with every round.

🕯️
The Curfew Show

Every night at a set time, all city lights go off. Only torches stay lit. Performers act out the fall of Porta Torre with fire juggling, sword fights, and a dramatic walk along the walls. No screens — just fire.

✦ Day One · Friday · AD 2026 ✦

The Drama

The Invasion Begins

The first day sets everything up. Visitors are sorted, passports are stamped, and the walls start to tell their story. When night comes, the city goes back to 1127 — with fire, theatre, and the old curfew darkness.

Entry System
The Medieval Passport
🚪
Porta Torre — The Invaders' Gate

Visitors who enter from the Milan side get the Invader stamp. Ticket price: €5 per person. Children under 12 and Como residents: free.

🏰
The Defenders' Entry

People already inside the old centre, or who enter by other gates, are stamped as Defenders of Como. Same ticket price. The difference is only for the mood — there is no game advantage.

🪙
Sesterzio Exchange

At the entrance, visitors exchange euros for Sesterzi Comaschi. These coins are used at all festival stalls. Leftover coins can be refunded at the exit or kept as a souvenir.

Official opening. First visitors get their passports. The AR wall experience is live. Silent Disco headphones are given out at the main entrance.

All food stalls at full speed. Aperitivo hour with local wine and food producers. Street music starts in the smaller squares.

All city lights go off. Only torches remain on the walls. Performers act out the fall of Porta Torre: fire juggling, sword fights, and a dramatic walk along the walls. Lasts about 45 minutes. This is the emotional heart of Day One.

Food stalls close. Sesterzio exchange booths reopen for refunds. Stewards guide visitors safely to all exits.
Special Feature
The Festival Map

Every visitor gets a paper map with their passport. The map shows all food and craft stalls with sesterzio prices, all QR code spots along the walls, audio guide zones, toilets, first aid points, emergency exits, and the stage for the evening fire show.

Logistics Note — The map comes in two versions: a simple black-and-white folded leaflet for mass distribution (~2,000 copies per day) and a larger illustrated poster version sold as a souvenir for €2.

✦ Day Two · Saturday · AD 2026 ✦

The Contest

War Becomes Excellence

The Milanese are inside the walls — but instead of swords they bring pots, recipes, and sporting energy. Saturday is the festival at full speed: every street has a stall, every square has a challenge, and every hour has a duel.

The Market
Streets of Stalls
🏪
Commercial Stalls Fee Required

Private sellers, craftspeople, and food makers pay a stall fee (€150–250 per day) to set up along the main streets. Categories: food, crafts, clothing, and local products. The number of stalls per type is limited to avoid too much repetition.

🏛️
Organiser Reserved Areas

Some spots are kept by the organisation for cooking challenge arenas, woodworking workshops, historical craft demos, and sponsor activities. These are all free to enter and watch.

🪵
Woodworking & Craft Labs

Workshops where visitors try medieval and Renaissance techniques: wood carving, silk weaving (a tribute to Como's famous textile history), and basic blacksmith demonstrations. Free to join, run by local craftspeople.

The Duels
Cooking Challenges · Como vs. Milan
Morning
🥐 Panettone (Milan) vs. Biascia (Como)
Teams from each city make their traditional sweet bread. A jury of 5 local food experts judges taste, tradition, and presentation. Tasting tickets are available. The public vote counts for 30% of the final score.
Midday
🍚 Risotto with Lake Fish (Como) vs. Ossobuco Milanese
The main cooking duel. Professional chefs cook at the same time on open-air stages. Live commentary by a food critic. Small tasting portions are sold to the public in sesterzi.
Evening
🍷 Secret Evening Challenge
The third challenge is only announced on the morning of Day Two. The likely format is a dessert or aperitivo duel, using ingredients from both areas. The surprise builds excitement.
The Tournaments
Sports Challenges
⚽
Football Tournament

5-a-side tournament in the afternoon. Open to youth and adult teams from Como and Milan. Sign up in advance on the festival website. Small entry fee per team (€20). Prize: trophy and festival vouchers.

🏹
Archery Contest

A traditional archery range near the walls. Open all day with free lessons. Afternoon competition for registered players. Medieval recurve bows are provided. Themed as the defence of Como in 1127.

🎯
Mini-Challenge Games

Relay races, tug of war, medieval ring toss, and sack races for children all through the day. Free, no booking needed. Volunteer helpers in period costume run the games.

Culture & Community
For Everyone
🎭
Traditional Como Theatre

Shows about Como's history through the centuries — dialect theatre, folk music, and storytelling. Easy to follow, made for families and older visitors. Two sessions: 15:00 and 17:30.

🕯️
The Curfew — Night 2

The torch curfew happens again at 21:00. This time the show is different — it is about the silence after the fall: a candlelit walk and a reading of historical accounts from both sides.

💡
Evening: DJ Set & Projections

From 22:00, a DJ plays in the main square. Projectors cast historical images and animations on the buildings and walls around the square. Stalls stay open. This is the biggest evening of the festival.

Estimated Attendance Day 2 — Saturday is expected to be the busiest day: between 3,000 and 4,500 visitors. All challenge arenas need crowd-management stewards. Food stall capacity should be tested before the event.

✦ Day Three · Sunday · AD 2026 ✦

The Fusion

Peace, Fire & One Table

The invasion is over. The contest has finished. What is left is a simple truth: Como and Milan were never really separated — two cities of the same world, connected by the lake, by silk, and by nine centuries of shared history. Sunday is the day of celebration, good food, and great light.

10:00
Gates Open — Final Day
All stalls reopen. The mood is warmer and more relaxed. Visitors who came all three days show their full passports — a shared identity has formed naturally over the weekend.
11:00 — 13:00
Volta Science Workshop Free
A demonstration of how to build a simple voltaic pile — Alessandro Volta's invention. Volta was born in Como, and his work changed the world. Sessions of 20 people, held 6 times during the morning.
11:30 — 13:00
Silk Weaving Workshop Free
Como has been famous for silk since the 1400s. Visitors try hand-weaving on a simple loom. Local craftspeople show old techniques from the time of the Visconti family. Educational but hands-on.
13:00 — 15:30
🍽️ La Tavolata Insubre — The Great Shared Meal
One long table running about 300–500 metres through the pedestrian centre. Thousands of visitors eat together and share typical products from Como and Milan: cheese, cured meats, bread, lake fish, and risotto. Food stalls serve at festival prices. The town and sponsors pay for a free starter tasting for everyone seated. A local musician plays acoustic music during the meal.
16:00 — 18:30
Last Market Hours & Award Ceremony
Final hours for all stalls. The results of the cooking challenges and sports events are announced in a short ceremony in the main square. Trophies, certificates, and applause from the crowd. The mayor or another public figure gives a closing speech.
19:00 — 21:00
🎥 Video Mapping: "One Engine"
The old walls of Como become a projection screen more than 200 metres wide. A 20-minute video mapping show plays every 45 minutes from sunset. The story: Como and Milan as one shared engine of culture — fashion, design, tourism, and new ideas. Modern images are layered over the medieval stone. Free to watch from the walls.
21:30
🎆 Grand Finale — Fireworks Show
The festival closes with a 12–15 minute fireworks show over the lake and the city walls. Synchronised to music that mixes medieval themes with modern orchestration. The last image: one firework in the shape of the Insubria region — Como and Milan as one.
The Heart of Day Three
La Tavolata Insubre

The Tavolata is the emotional heart of the whole festival. A shared table 300–500 metres long runs through the pedestrian historic centre, set with linen, candles, and shared plates. Similar events have worked very well in other Italian cities (Tavola di San Giuseppe, Slow Food events) and are fully doable with good planning.

📐 Logistics

About 500 metres of tables, with seats for 800–1,200 people at a time. The municipality must approve street closures. Tables are rented from event companies. Volunteers manage setup and clearing.

🧀 Food Model

Visitors buy food tickets in sesterzi in advance or at the entrance. Food stations are every 50 metres along the table. Local producers sell at cost for visibility. Sponsors fund a free starter for all seated guests.

📸 Visual Impact

Drone footage of the meal becomes the festival's main media content. One aerial shot of the full table — with the old walls behind and the lake in the distance — explains the whole festival concept in one single image.

Day 3 Tone — This day is deliberately slower and more personal than Day 2. The goal is not the biggest crowd but the strongest memory. Families, older visitors, and people who came for all three days should feel that this is the reward they came for.

Projected Balance

+ €4,750 surplus

Based on the careful scenario: 60% paying visitor rate, 40 stalls, 4 sponsors. A 10% contingency fund is already included in the cost total. The surplus can fund a second edition of the festival or be donated to a local heritage conservation fund.

Key Risks — (1) Bad weather: rain on Day 2 could cut attendance by 40%. A covered backup stage is a good idea. (2) Fireworks need approval from the Prefettura at least 60 days before the event. (3) The AR system is a one-time expense — for future editions the cost is almost zero. (4) The sesterzio exchange needs a clear refund policy to avoid complaints.
Municipal Partnership — The municipality's involvement is essential — not just for money, but because street closure permits, police coordination, and access to the historic walls all need official approval. Present this festival as a cultural tourism project to access the municipality's promotional channels and funding.
Sponsor Package Levels — Gold (€3,000+): naming rights for one arena, logo on passport and map, stage banner. Silver (€1,500): logo on map and signs, mention in announcements. Bronze (€500): logo on programme and website. In-kind: product donation in exchange for stall visibility.
